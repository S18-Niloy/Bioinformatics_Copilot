DNA (deoxyribonucleic acid) is a double-stranded molecule composed of nucleotides containing adenine (A), thymine (T), guanine (G), and cytosine (C). A pairs with T, and G pairs with C.
RNA (ribonucleic acid) is typically single-stranded and uses uracil (U) instead of thymine. Messenger RNA carries the genetic code from DNA to the ribosome where it is translated into protein.
The central dogma of molecular biology states that genetic information flows from DNA to RNA to protein via transcription and translation.
An open reading frame (ORF) is a sequence of DNA that starts with a start codon (ATG) and ends with a stop codon (TAA, TAG, TGA) without intervening stop codons.
GC content is the percentage of guanine and cytosine bases in a DNA sequence. High GC content correlates with higher melting temperature and is common in thermophilic organisms.
Proteins are polymers of amino acids encoded by triplets of nucleotides called codons. There are 20 standard amino acids.
BLAST (Basic Local Alignment Search Tool) is the most widely used algorithm for searching sequence databases for similar sequences.
Pairwise sequence alignment aligns two sequences to identify regions of similarity. Global alignment (Needleman-Wunsch) aligns the entire length; local alignment (Smith-Waterman) aligns the most similar subregions.
Multiple sequence alignment aligns three or more sequences and is used to infer evolutionary relationships.
Hemoglobin is the iron-containing oxygen-transport metalloprotein in red blood cells. It consists of four globin subunits (two alpha and two beta in adults).
A gene is a sequence of DNA that encodes a functional product, usually a protein or a non-coding RNA.
Transcription factors are proteins that bind to specific DNA sequences to control the rate of transcription of genetic information from DNA to messenger RNA.
A graph neural network (GNN) is a class of neural network that operates on graph-structured data. GCN (Graph Convolutional Network) and GAT (Graph Attention Network) are common variants used in computational biology for protein-protein interaction prediction and drug discovery.
Protein-protein interaction (PPI) networks model proteins as nodes and physical interactions as edges. Predicting node labels (e.g. function) is a common GNN task.
Retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) combines a vector retriever (e.g. FAISS) with a generative language model: relevant passages are retrieved and provided as context to the LLM.
Embeddings are dense vector representations of biological sequences (or text) such that similar inputs map to nearby vectors. Sentence-transformers and ESM protein language models are common sources.
EGFR (Epidermal Growth Factor Receptor) is a receptor tyrosine kinase frequently mutated or overexpressed in cancers, and a major drug target.
BRCA1 is a tumor suppressor gene; loss-of-function mutations substantially increase the risk of breast and ovarian cancer.
Collagen is the most abundant protein in mammals and a major component of connective tissue.
Serum albumin is the most abundant protein in human blood plasma and transports many ligands.
AlphaFold is a deep learning system developed by DeepMind for predicting protein three-dimensional structure from amino acid sequence with high accuracy, enabling structure-based insights in biology and drug discovery.
